Attributes

HammRPG uses six common attributes to define a character's general affinity with certain abilities: Constitution (CON), Strength (STR), Dexterity (DEX), Intelligence (INT), Wisdom (WIS) and Charisma (CHA).

Attributes are used in two different ways:

  1. The attribute value itself.
  2. An ability modifier based on that attribute.

For all attributes, HammRPG uses the full name (e.g.: Constitution) when referring to the attribute itself, while we use use the abbreviation (e.g.: CON) when referring to the modifier.

Attribute Point Distribution

There are various ways to allocate Attribute Points (AP) in your specific game. Which system you use should be discussed during the Session Zero before you actually start playing.

The main method of attribute point distribution endorsed by HammRPG is sometimes called Point-Buy: All characters start with 60 Attribute Points (AP), and gain 1 additional AP per Lv. These points can be allocated to any of the attributes entirely at the player's preference. This is the default method, as it allows complete player freedon in their character creation, and results in characters that should be at least reasonably similar in power compared to each other. Of course, in a game as customizable as HammRPG, complete balance is nearly impossible, as one set of abilities might synergize better than another.

Another popular method is Stat-Rolling: Each player rolls three six-sided dice (3d6) six times. The results of the rolls are either appended to the attributes in order, or at the player's preference, per group discussion. This method introduces an element of randomness, which can result in wildly different characters strength levels. While experienced players may relish the challenge of adjusting their play-style according to what character they rolled, newer players may find it more difficult, especially if they ended up with a below average character. Additionally, most enemies in the GM guide are balanced around the idea that characters will have a base of 60 AP.

Note that AP (as well as most other types of points) can only be changed when the character is created, or during level-up events, which usually require a full rest.

Attribute Modifiers

Modifiers are calculated based on the attribute most relevant to the roll. An attribute score of 10 is considered average, resulting in no modifiers to rolls based on that attribute. If a character has an attribute score lower than 10, they will have a negative modifier to those rolls, while an attribute higher than 10 will grant a bonus to those rolls.

The formula for calculating your modifier is:

modifier = ( attribute - 10 ) / 2

Note: Only integer values are valid for modifier calculations. A result of less than 1 is always rounded down to the nearest full number.

E.g. if a character has an attribute value of 11, their modifier is (11-10)/2=0.5 => 0.

Constitution

Constitution (CON) is a measure of a character's toughness, and their ability to withstand damage. The most direct way to represent this is with the number of Health Points (HP) the character has:

maxHP = Constitution * Lv

Based on this formula, you can see that maxHP increases with higher values of Constitution, as well as the character level itself. As such, a character with high Constitution will continue to gain larger amounts of maxHP per level-up than one with low Constitution.

Constitution also affects Physical Resistance (PR), which further helps your character withstand damage. The PR modifier works the same as the regular CON modifier, except that it does not have negative values, even if your Constitutionis less than 10.

If CON < 0:

PR = 0

If CON >= 0:

PR = CON

Please refer to the damage calculation guide below for further details on PR.

The Endurance and Resistance abilities are also based on Constitution.

Strength

Strength (STR) is a measure of the physical force that a character can exert. One of its largest benefits is its effect on Melee Damage (MD), which will cause your melee attacks to hit harder. Just as PR does not have negative values, MD also does not decrease a damage roll's (dmg) result.

If STR < 0:

MD = 0

If STR >= 0:

MD = STR

Please refer to the damage calculation guide below for further details on MD.

Strength also affects the Athletics, Grappling and Intimidation abilities.

Dexterity

Dexterity (DEX) is a measure of agility and flexibility. A character with a high Dexterity attribute is better able to perform jumps and other complicated maneuvers, avoid or disable traps, and may even be able to dodge incoming attacks entirely.

Dodge Value (DV), which allows characters to completely avoid attacks, is calculated similar to other modifiers, however, it scales only half as rapidly:

DV = ( DEX - 10 ) / 4

It is important to note that DV does not make a character invulnerable: Area damage, such as an exploding fireball, a cloud of poison, a collapsing wall, or a shockwave from an artillery shell, applies directly, without any chance to dodge. In such cases, the GM may call for a Reflex or Resistance save to reduce the damage, but DV itself will not come into play.

Dexterity also affects the Acrobatics, Reflex, Stealth and Thievery abilities.

Intelligence

Intelligence (INT) represents a character's ability to use their mind through their Mana Points (MP):

maxMP = Intelligence * Lv

As you can see, the formula is the same as the formula for maxHP, except instead of using Constitution as a base, MP are based on INT. As MP are needed to use active abilities, this means that characters with a high Intelligence attribute will be able to use their active abilities more often than those with low Intelligence.

Intelligence also comes into play as a form of roleplay immersion. For instance, an NPC with a low Intelligence attribute is less likely to use advanced combat tactics than one with high Intelligence. Many players also enjoy roleplaying these details, as it creates a fun atmosphere for everyone.

The abilities based on Intelligence focus more on roleplaying than on pure combat stats, through Crafting, History, Investigation, and Mindfulness. However, a smart player can still use this attribute to create a powerful character.

Wisdom

Wisdom (WIS) represents a character's attunement with the world around them, including magic. Most magic based abilities use the WIS modifier to enhance magical damage rolls, and a high Wisdom attribute also improves magical reisitance (MR) to defend against enemy spells. The formula is very similar to that for PR:

If WIS < 0:

MR = 0

If WIS >= 0:

MR = WIS

Therefore, a character with a Wisdom attribute of 12, would automatically have an MR value of +1. Please refer to the damage calculation guide below for further details on MR.

Wisdom further affects the ability rolls for Intuition, Perception, Survival and Willpower.

Charisma

Charisma (CHA) is the attribute least applicable to combat, but that does not make it any weaker than the other attributes. A character with high Charisma benefits from significant advantages during roleplay elements of a game, including the potential to avoid combat altogether. Meanwhile, a character with a particularly low Charisma score may find themselves unfortunately vulnerable to spells that affect the mind, which range from card tricks and other small illusions to game-changing charms or mind-controls. Nevertheless, Charisma remains one of the subtler attributes.

Groups who do not enjoy the haggling experience in their games can also use the CHA modifier to quickly calculate discounts when their characters visit merchants to purchase items:

When Purchasing:

Purchase Price
=
Armory Price * ( 1.5 - ( CHA / 50 ) ) * Local Supply Modifier

When Selling:

Selling Price
=
Armory Price * ( 0.5 + ( CHA / 50 ) ) * Local Supply Modifier

The Inspiration, Leadership, Performance, and Persuasion abilities are based on Charisma.
